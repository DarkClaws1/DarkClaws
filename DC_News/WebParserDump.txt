										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Relationship trouble comes in all forms, but one man took to Reddit with a rather unusual situation: He was jealous of his girlfriend&#8217;s dog. </p>
<p>According to him, his girlfriend put more effort into the birthday celebration for her dog than she did for him and it made him feel some type of way. </p>

<p>The man, Reddit user <a href="https://www.reddit.com/user/Sensitive-Home2524/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">u/Sensitive-Home2524</a>, is now unsure of his relationship and took to the platform&#8217;s &#8220;<a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/relationship_advice/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Relationship Advice</a>&#8221; sub for advice. </p>
<p>He <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/relationship_advice/comments/ws4jem/girlfriend24f_went_more_all_out_for_her_dogs_2f/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">wrote</a>, &#8220;We&#8217;ve been together for 2 years and each year, she hasn&#8217;t typically been able to get me anything due to financial reasons. While disappointing, I always understood and don&#8217;t necessarily expect anything on my birthday regardless.&#8221;</p>
<p>However, his understanding changed after his girlfriend adopted a dog and suddenly came up with money to spend. </p>

<p>He said, &#8220;About 1 year ago she adopted a dog who is like a child to her. Her (the dog&#8217;s) birthday just happens to be a couple of weeks away from mine.&#8221; He went on to explain that on his birthday, his girlfriend got him a stuffed animal (though he&#8217;d previously expressed he didn&#8217;t like stuffed animals). <p>Needless to say, he was feeling rather hurt seeing the money and effort his partner put into her dog&#8217;s birthday compared to his. </p>

<p>He added that for his girlfriend&#8217;s birthday, he took her out to dinner and got her a new tank for an aquarium she has going. </p>

<p>As Stephanie <a href="https://www.tiktok.com/@thisismycircus0/video/7136578398209510699" rel="noopener" target="_blank">shared</a> on TikTok, Waffles heroically saved the life of his goat friend, Toro, when Toro&#8217;s neck became trapped in the fence. </p>

<p>Goats are known for getting their heads stuck in things and it leaves them susceptible to injuries and predators, so time is of the essence in freeing them &#8211; especially at night. </p>
<p>It seems Waffles sensed the urgency of the situation and when Toro&#8217;s head became trapped around 12:30AM, he stood outside Stephanie&#8217;s door and &#8220;screamed&#8221; at her until she came outside. </p>

<p>The post <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com/queen-elizabeth-ii-first-loves-horses-corgis/">All About Queen Elizabeth II&#8217;s First Loves &#8211; Horses &#038; Corgis</a> appeared first on <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com">The Animal Rescue Site News</a>.</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Queen Elizabeth II ruled for 70 years making her the longest-reigning British monarch. She passed away yesterday at the age of 96 surrounded by loved ones, which most likely included her beloved dogs. </p>
<p>The Queen has always loved horses and dogs and they remained a big part of her life throughout her reign. They were her first loves. She managed to find time to ride her trusty steeds and was often seen attending horse events or out in the garden with her dogs. </p>
<p>As the world grieves for the Her Majesty, we thought we would take a look back and see when the Queen first fell in love with horses and learn when corgis first entered her home. </p>
<h3> All the Queen&#8217;s Horses</h3>
<p>She received her first horse, a Shetland pony named Peggy, when she was just 4 years old. After successfully learning how to stay on the pony, she continued riding and by the age of 18 was an accomplished rider. </p>
<p><iframe loading="lazy" src="https://www.facebook.com/plugins/post.php?href=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2FTheBritishMonarchy%2Fphotos%2Fa.740720565949776%2F3110828368938972%2F%3Ftype%3D3&#038;show_text=false&#038;width=500" width="500" height="312" style="border:none;overflow:hidden" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="true" allow="autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; picture-in-picture; web-share"></iframe></p>
<p>Her passion for horses didn&#8217;t fade and only grew with time. Over the years she has owned and bred many horses. She personally named each one and loved them all dearly. However, some held a special place in heart. Horse &#038; Hound <a href="https://www.horseandhound.co.uk/features/the-queen-favourite-horses-hh-plus-716763" rel="noopener" target="_blank">released</a> an exclusive article in 2020 naming the Queen&#8217;s favorites. In no particular order, they were:</p>
<p><iframe loading="lazy" src="https://www.facebook.com/plugins/post.php?href=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2FTheBritishMonarchy%2Fphotos%2Fa.740720565949776%2F3426924673996005%2F%3Ftype%3D3&#038;show_text=false&#038;width=500" width="500" height="497" style="border:none;overflow:hidden" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="true" allow="autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; picture-in-picture; web-share"></iframe></p>
<ul>
<li>Betsy: a black-brown mare that the Queen rode in the 1960s. </li>
<li>Burmese: presented to Her Majesty in 1969 by The Royal Canadian Mounted Police, and The Queen rode her in Trooping the Colour until 1986. </li>
<li>Doublet: the horse bred by the Queen and ridden by Princess Ann. The duo won the European Eventing Championships in 1971. </li>
<li>Columbus: 17.1hh grey sired by Winston Churchill&#8217;s stallion Colonist. While his barn name was &#8220;The Monster&#8221;, the Queen adored him and said he was the &#8220;Grand National horse that never was&#8221;.</li>
<li>Sanction: the brown riding horse was a favorite of the Queen’s for many years. </li>
<li>Balmoral Jingle and Balmoral Curlew: two beautiful Highland mares.  </li>
<li>Emma: a Fell pony who also was one of the Queen&#8217;s favorite riding ponies. </li>
<li>Aureole: the chestnut stallion was an amazing racehorse and the first horse the Queen inherited from her late father. </li>
<li>Doutelle: chestnut stallion bred by the Queen who went on to be a winning racehorse.  </li>
<li>Highclere: bay mare who won both the 1000 Guineas and Prix de Diane in Paris. </li>
<li>Phantom Gold: amazing racehorse who went on to produce many stakes winners. </li>
<li>Estimate: a beautiful thoroughbred mare who won the Ascot Gold Cup at the Royal meeting at the age of 4.</li>
</ul>

He was listening to everything the therapist instructed him to do.</p>

Those people are the ones who have been taking care of animals since they were kids. Their love for animals knows no bounds — being a pet parent has been their calling. It’s lovely that pet parents chose a road to provide hope for animals — giving them the life they deserve.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.pexels.com/photo/woman-in-sweater-holding-orange-and-white-cat-6865039/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><figure id="attachment_389661" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-389661" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/woman-squirrel-in-line-1.jpg" alt="" width="1000" height="523" class="size-full wp-image-389661" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/woman-squirrel-in-line-1.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/woman-squirrel-in-line-1-600x314.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/woman-squirrel-in-line-1-300x157.jpg 300w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/woman-squirrel-in-line-1-768x402.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-389661" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels/cottonbro</figcaption></figure></a></p>
<p>But sometimes, a person can unexpectedly turn into a pet parent. They spent time planning the future, and pet adoption did not even cross their mind. Life just amazingly led them to someone — an animal that will bring positive changes with love. They did not choose the pet parenting life; it chose them. Interestingly, Cindy had a similar experience that she shared with GeoBeats Animals. Her life went completely different when she suddenly became a full-time pet mom. More surprisingly, it wasn’t a cat or dog; she found a squirrel.</p>

<p>“One evening, my boyfriend and I were driving home, and we saw something on the road. It was a baby squirrel, and she was wet,” Cindy narrated. “It was pretty late in the day, and it was chilly outside. So I took Sammy home.” Since then, her pet parenting journey began. Although Cindy had no idea about caring for a baby squirrel, she did her best through research. The baby squirrel she named Sammy just found a warm home — meeting Cindy was serendipitous. </p>

<p>“A girl on Instagram actually sent me a message about him. She had found this young male squirrel and needed to find somebody that could take him,” Cindy shared. Cindy&#8217;s adorable squirrels are now the best of friends.</p>

<p>The Queen&#8217;s death comes after she had stepped back from many events in recent months due to mobility issues. She had also lost her husband of 73 years, Prince Philip, the Duke of Edinburgh, last year.</p>
<p>Queen Elizabeth II was born April 21, 1926 in London to Prince Albert, Duke of York, and Lady Elizabeth Bowes-Lyon. Her father was the second son of the reigning king, George V, but when his older brother, Edward VIII, abdicated in 1936, the young princess became heir to the throne.</p>
<p>She began her life of service to her country at a young age, <a href="https://www.nationalww2museum.org/war/articles/queen-elizabeth-ii-during-world-war-ii">serving in</a> the Auxiliary Territorial Service of the British Army during World War II. Joining at 18, she worked as a mechanic. Earlier on in the war, at the age of 14, she had also shared an encouraging message that was broadcast over radio to other children who had been evacuated away from their families for safety.</p>
<p>She ascended to the throne on February 6, 1952, upon the death of her father, King George VI. She was 25 at the time, and a mother to two young children. <p>So what comes next? The protocol is contained in &#8220;Operation London Bridge.&#8221;</p>
<p>When a monarch dies, a code has often been used to communicate the fact. In Queen Elizabeth&#8217;s case, that code was &#8220;London Bridge is down.&#8221; Once that code was relayed to the prime minister, the news was shared with the governments of the Commonwealth nations and the nations outside the UK for which the Queen serves as head of state. After that, the public learned the news.</p>
<p>That was accomplished in two primary ways: A footman dressed in black pinned the death announcement to Buckingham Palace&#8217;s gates, and a news alert was sent to global media simultaneously. The palace also communicated the message on its website, which reverted to a single page with a dark background.</p>
<p>With flags lowered to honor the Queen on the day of her death, they will be raised the next morning. At that time, Charles III will officially be named King and his wife Camilla will officially become Queen Consort. The new monarch will then embark on a tour of the UK.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the Queen will lie in state at Westminster Hall ahead of her funeral, which will be held on the 10th day after her death. This occasion will be marked with a national day of mourning. She will be buried in St. George&#8217;s Chapel at Windsor Castle in the King George VI Memorial Chapel, with her parents, sister Princess Margaret, and husband Prince Philip.</p>
