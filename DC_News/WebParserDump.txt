										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>In Rural Retreat, Virginia, a close-knit family, the Deels, is dealing with the heart-wrenching disappearance of their beloved bear hound, Ringo. Three women, driving a maroon minivan with Florida plates, are at the center of this tale.</p>
<p>For the Deel family, hunting is more than just a pastime; it&#8217;s a way of life. As <a href="https://www.outdoorlife.com/hunting/bear-hound-stolen-in-virginia/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Outdoor Life</a> reports, Rocky Deel and his 11-year-old son Charlie share a deep bond with their dogs, which are essential for bear hunting. The Deels&#8217; dogs are specially bred for the task, and they&#8217;ve never had any conflicts with others in their tight-knit hunting community.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430524" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430524" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" fetchpriority="high" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-6.jpg" alt="Ringo, a "blue English" coonhound, went missing after a day of bear hunting." width="1000" height="1414" class="size-full wp-image-430524" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-6.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-6-239x338.jpg 239w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-6-477x675.jpg 477w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-6-120x169.jpg 120w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-6-768x1086.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430524" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/996390651634262/posts/997805884826072/?__cft__[0]=AZX70PWHgwdIfF6cksfr0BoZwopGH2nwfMuQ2AB3f9zvgcgIXnzNUJqr7cqRMxcQJEwee3bWJcXgyoXabncd4YtnZg0YCxJ9mqy78dWxC9V3HUOhtyT2W_QwH65rjLO73H9GzXouaMT1XZhGWYb48x-Bj-iAxZCUKGta08iWQo-NkvwdSQSnUJ8ASL5S8sEUlg1Pub2PnNoO7did2p7Z6T47&#038;__tn__=%2CO%2CP-R" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Facebook / Sierra Whalen</a><br />Ringo, a &#8220;blue English&#8221; coonhound, went missing after a day of bear hunting.</figcaption></figure>
<p>During a local youth hunting tournament in October, the Deels faced an unexpected twist. After a day of bear hunting, the family discovered that Ringo, a &#8220;blue English&#8221; coonhound, had gone missing. </p>
<p>It was a shock to the family because these dogs are typically capable of tracking animals from 10 to 20 miles away.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430525" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430525" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-5.jpg" alt="The women who allegedly took Ringo are driving a maroon minivan with Florida plates." width="1000" height="1316" class="size-full wp-image-430525" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-5.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-5-257x338.jpg 257w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-5-513x675.jpg 513w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-5-128x169.jpg 128w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-5-768x1011.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430525" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/996390651634262/posts/997805884826072/?__cft__[0]=AZX70PWHgwdIfF6cksfr0BoZwopGH2nwfMuQ2AB3f9zvgcgIXnzNUJqr7cqRMxcQJEwee3bWJcXgyoXabncd4YtnZg0YCxJ9mqy78dWxC9V3HUOhtyT2W_QwH65rjLO73H9GzXouaMT1XZhGWYb48x-Bj-iAxZCUKGta08iWQo-NkvwdSQSnUJ8ASL5S8sEUlg1Pub2PnNoO7did2p7Z6T47&#038;__tn__=%2CO%2CP-R" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Facebook / Sierra Whalen</a><br />The women who allegedly took Ringo are driving a maroon minivan with Florida plates.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>A Puzzling Phone Call</h3>
<p>The Deels&#8217; initial hope turned into worry when they received a call from another hunter. This individual reported seeing Ringo with two people on the side of a highway. Despite the hunt&#8217;s high stakes and the family&#8217;s deep connection to their hunting dogs, Ringo&#8217;s well-being was suddenly uncertain.</p>
<p>The women with Ringo claimed they were &#8220;saving&#8221; him from what they perceived as a neglectful hunting environment. </p>
<figure id="attachment_430522" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430522" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8.jpg" alt="Surveillance video caught a glimpse of the alleged dognappers." width="1000" height="2057" class="size-full wp-image-430522" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8-164x338.jpg 164w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8-328x675.jpg 328w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8-82x169.jpg 82w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8-768x1580.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8-747x1536.jpg 747w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-8-996x2048.jpg 996w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430522" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/996390651634262/posts/997805884826072/?__cft__[0]=AZX70PWHgwdIfF6cksfr0BoZwopGH2nwfMuQ2AB3f9zvgcgIXnzNUJqr7cqRMxcQJEwee3bWJcXgyoXabncd4YtnZg0YCxJ9mqy78dWxC9V3HUOhtyT2W_QwH65rjLO73H9GzXouaMT1XZhGWYb48x-Bj-iAxZCUKGta08iWQo-NkvwdSQSnUJ8ASL5S8sEUlg1Pub2PnNoO7did2p7Z6T47&#038;__tn__=%2CO%2CP-R" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Facebook / Sierra Whalen</a><br />Surveillance video caught a glimpse of the alleged dognappers.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Bear hunting has faced opposition from anti-hunting activists in the past. However, the Deels emphasize that their dogs are bred for hunting, and they don&#8217;t force them into it. They believe that these women misunderstood their relationship with Ringo.</p>
<p>“We just miss him, he was one of my best hounds,” Deel told <a href="https://www.wsls.com/news/local/2023/10/18/hunting-dog-stolen-from-rural-retreat-family/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">WSLS</a>. “We raised him from a puppy. You miss him, he’s like part of the family.”</p>

<h3>Legal Consequences for Stealing Ringo</h3>
<p>The three women may not have considered the legal implications of their actions. <a href="https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title18.2/chapter5/section18.2-97/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Stealing a dog in Virginia</a> is a Class 5 felony, carrying a penalty of up to 10 years in prison. Additionally, removing a GPS collar from a dog is a Class 1 misdemeanor, which could lead to another year in jail or a $2,500 fine. Further, interference with lawful hunting is a Class 3 misdemeanor, which may result in a $500 fine. </p>
<figure id="attachment_430523" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430523" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-7.jpg" alt="The Deel family is offering a reward for anyone who can help them find their dog." width="1000" height="1778" class="size-full wp-image-430523" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-7.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-7-190x338.jpg 190w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-7-380x675.jpg 380w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-7-95x169.jpg 95w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-7-768x1366.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/stolen-dog-ringo-7-864x1536.jpg 864w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430523" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/996390651634262/posts/997805884826072/?__cft__[0]=AZX70PWHgwdIfF6cksfr0BoZwopGH2nwfMuQ2AB3f9zvgcgIXnzNUJqr7cqRMxcQJEwee3bWJcXgyoXabncd4YtnZg0YCxJ9mqy78dWxC9V3HUOhtyT2W_QwH65rjLO73H9GzXouaMT1XZhGWYb48x-Bj-iAxZCUKGta08iWQo-NkvwdSQSnUJ8ASL5S8sEUlg1Pub2PnNoO7did2p7Z6T47&#038;__tn__=%2CO%2CP-R" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Facebook / Sierra Whalen</a><br />The Deel family is offering a reward for anyone who can help them find their dog.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Relentless Search and a Reward</h3>
<p>In response to Ringo&#8217;s disappearance, the Deels have taken extensive measures to find their beloved hound. They&#8217;ve created a <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/996390651634262" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Facebook page</a> to gather information and have filed police reports. They&#8217;ve reached out to dog rescue organizations and shelters in the area, leaving no stone unturned in their search for Ringo.</p>
<p>Rules regarding animal welfare may differ from state to state, but there are always legal consequences for pet theft. As the Deels continue their relentless search, their story resonates with pet lovers, hunters, and anyone who understands the depth of the bond between humans and their furry companions.</p>

<h3>What to Do When You Encounter a Bobcat</h3>
<p>While these encounters with bobcats are undoubtedly heartwarming and fascinating, it&#8217;s essential to remember that bobcats are wild animals. As <a href="https://www.advnture.com/features/what-to-do-bobcat-hiking" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Advnture</a> advises, if you ever find yourself in a situation where you encounter one of these beautiful felines, here&#8217;s what you should do:</p>
<h4>7. Maintain a Respectful Distance</h4>
<p>Bobcats are naturally wary of humans, and it&#8217;s crucial to maintain a safe distance. Avoid approaching them, as they may become defensive if they feel threatened.</p>
<h4>6. Do Not Feed Bobcats</h4>
<p>Feeding wild animals can lead to habituation, making bobcats more comfortable around humans. This can be dangerous for both humans and the bobcats, as it disrupts their natural behaviors.</p>
<h4>5. Respect Their Space</h4>
<p>Give bobcats plenty of space to move freely. If you spot a bobcat nearby, step back and provide it with an avenue to escape. Avoid cornering them.</p>
<h4>4. Keep Pets Indoors</h4>
<p>If you have pets, especially small dogs or cats, ensure they are kept indoors, as bobcats may see them as potential prey.</p>
<h4>3. Make Noise</h4>
<p>In the event of a bobcat encounter, and if it doesn&#8217;t immediately retreat, making loud noises can help scare it away.</p>
<h4>2. Avoid Turning Your Back</h4>
<p>If a bobcat is nearby, maintain eye contact and back away slowly without turning your back, which can be perceived as a sign of vulnerability.</p>
<h4>1. Report Abnormal Behavior</h4>
<p>If you notice a bobcat acting unusually or displaying aggressive behavior, contact your local wildlife authorities. They can provide guidance and assistance as needed.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430504" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430504" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bobcat-encounter-tucson-8.jpg" alt="Feeding bobcats can lead to habituation and should be avoided." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-430504" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bobcat-encounter-tucson-8.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bobcat-encounter-tucson-8-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bobcat-encounter-tucson-8-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bobcat-encounter-tucson-8-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430504" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Feeding bobcats can lead to habituation and should be avoided.</figcaption></figure>
<p>While adorable baby bobcat encounters are heartwarming and captivating, it&#8217;s essential to appreciate these encounters from a respectful distance. Bobcats are wild creatures, and their behavior can be unpredictable. By adhering to safety guidelines, we can ensure that both bobcats and humans coexist peacefully, allowing us to marvel at the beauty of these remarkable animals while safeguarding their well-being.</p>
<p>Click below and together, we can ensure that encounters with wildlife in danger lead to positive outcomes, safeguarding the beauty and diversity of the animal kingdom for generations to come.</p>

<p>The post <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com/golden-retriever-longevity/">Scientists Discover Gene That Extends Golden Retrievers&#8217; Lives And Could Help Humans</a> appeared first on <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com">The Animal Rescue Site News</a>.</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Golden retrievers, one of the most beloved canine companions in the world, often face a heartbreaking statistic: up to a 65% chance of succumbing to cancer. The prevalence of this deadly disease in golden retrievers prompted researchers at the University of California, Davis to delve into the breed&#8217;s genetic makeup to identify a potential key to longer and healthier lives. Their groundbreaking study, recently published in the journal <a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11357-023-00968-2" target="_blank" rel="noopener">GeroScience</a>, has uncovered a surprising genetic discovery that holds hope not only for these cherished dogs but also for humans facing similar cancer risks.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430495" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430495" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-6.jpg" alt="Golden Retrievers are one of the most popular dog breeds globally." width="1000" height="1333" class="size-full wp-image-430495" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-6.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-6-254x338.jpg 254w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-6-506x675.jpg 506w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-6-127x169.jpg 127w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-6-768x1024.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430495" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Golden Retrievers are one of the most popular dog breeds globally.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>A Puzzling Predicament</h3>
<p>These golden retrievers, beloved for their friendly demeanor and golden coats, suffer a disproportionately high cancer risk. While some live to a ripe old age, others are tragically cut short by this devastating disease. As <a href="https://metro.co.uk/2023/10/20/golden-retriever-dogs-could-hold-the-secret-to-beating-cancer-19697141/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Metro</a> reports, the researchers pondered whether there might be a hidden genetic factor contributing to the differences in their lifespans &#8212; could genetics be the missing piece to this perplexing puzzle?</p>
<h3>The Discovery of HER4</h3>
<p>Rather than searching for genes linked to cancer diagnoses in golden retrievers, the UC Davis researchers decided to take an alternative approach. They set out to uncover genes associated with longevity instead. According to <a href="https://phys.org/news/2023-10-golden-longer-gene-longevity.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Phys.org</a>, the gene that emerged from their exhaustive study is known as HER4, or ERBB4. It belongs to the same family of human epidermal growth factor receptors as HER2, infamous for its role in promoting rapid cancer cell growth.</p>
<p>HER4 has a critical role to play not only in human cancer but also in canine health, Phys.org reports. This discovery offers a glimmer of hope for both golden retrievers and potentially for humans, as the two species share several types of cancers.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430492" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430492" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-9.jpg" alt="Golden Retrievers have a strong work ethic and love to please their owners." width="1000" height="1333" class="size-full wp-image-430492" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-9.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-9-254x338.jpg 254w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-9-506x675.jpg 506w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-9-127x169.jpg 127w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-9-768x1024.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430492" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Golden Retrievers have a strong work ethic and love to please their owners.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Extending the Lives of Our Beloved Pets</h3>
<p>To reach their findings, the researchers collected DNA samples from over 300 golden retrievers. They meticulously compared the DNA of dogs that reached 14 years of age with those that sadly succumbed to cancer before they turned 12. The results were nothing short of astonishing. According to <a href="https://www.newsweek.com/golden-retrievers-secret-longer-life-gene-cancer-1836484" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Newsweek</a>, dogs harboring specific variants of the HER4 gene enjoyed nearly two extra years of life, with an average lifespan of 13.5 years compared to 11.6 years.</p>
<p>This groundbreaking discovery represents a significant breakthrough. As Phys.org reports, in additional two years in a dog&#8217;s life is equivalent to a 15-20% increase in lifespan, akin to 12-14 human years. It&#8217;s an outcome that every pet owner dreams of—more cherished time with their loyal and loving companions.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430496" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430496" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-5.jpg" alt="Golden Retrievers are often used as therapy and service dogs." width="1000" height="1500" class="size-full wp-image-430496" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-5.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-5-225x338.jpg 225w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-5-450x675.jpg 450w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-5-113x169.jpg 113w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/golden-retriever-longevity-5-768x1152.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430496" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Golden Retrievers are often used as therapy and service dogs.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>A Gendered Genetic Impact</h3>
<p>The research also uncovered intriguing gender differences. As covered in another study published in <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5861885/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The Journals of Gerontology</a>, HER4 seemed to have a more pronounced impact on the longevity of female dogs compared to their male counterparts. This gendered variation may be attributed to the gene&#8217;s interaction with hormones, especially estrogen.</p>
<h3>The Promise of Future Studies</h3>
<p>While this discovery is a ray of hope for golden retrievers and potentially for humans, it&#8217;s just the first step in a long journey to unravel the complexities of cancer in these canine companions. Researchers acknowledge that numerous genes are likely involved in the interplay, and HER4 may be just one piece of the puzzle.</p>
<p>To shed further light on this genetic marvel, the next crucial step involves conducting studies with a more extensive population of golden retrievers. This will help verify and replicate the results and, more importantly, unveil how the genetic variants influence the expression and function of the HER4 gene.</p>

<h3>Bridging the Canine-Human Gap</h3>
<p>The connection between golden retrievers and human cancer is a remarkable revelation. As researched in a study published in <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8659013/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Molecules</a>, the implications of HER4’s role in the formation and progression of cancer, as well as its potential to modify cancer risk, are promising for future cancer studies in both dogs and humans. This genetic revelation could pave the way for novel interventions and treatments, giving hope to those battling cancer on both sides of the species divide.</p>
<p>Golden retrievers, beloved for their warmth and affection, may now become beacons of hope in the fight against cancer. The discovery of the HER4 gene and its potential to extend lives offers a glimmer of optimism for these canine companions and a source of inspiration for the ongoing battle against cancer in the wider world.</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Climate change is affecting not only humans but also our furry friends. As global temperatures continue to rise, certain dog breeds face unique challenges that can be life-threatening. Bulldogs, pugs, and Boston terriers, affectionately known as brachycephalic breeds, are particularly vulnerable to the changing climate.</p>
<p>Understanding the reasons behind the susceptibility of various dog breeds to climate change is one step in ensuring their future well-being.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430463" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430463" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-8.jpg" alt="Climate change affects pets as rising temperatures can lead to heat-related illnesses and discomfort in pets." width="1000" height="1500" class="size-full wp-image-430463" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-8.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-8-225x338.jpg 225w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-8-450x675.jpg 450w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-8-113x169.jpg 113w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-8-768x1152.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430463" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Climate change affects pets as rising temperatures can lead to heat-related illnesses and discomfort in pets.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Why Are Brachycephalic Breeds at Risk?</h3>
<p><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5508944/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Brachycephalic dogs</a> have distinctive flat faces and short snouts, a look achieved through selective breeding for aesthetic purposes rather than functionality. This breeding trend has left these breeds with narrowed nostrils, elongated soft palates, and narrowed windpipes, limiting their ability to thermoregulate effectively, reports <a href="https://news.vt.edu/articles/2023/09/dogs_climate_change_expert.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Virginia Tech</a>. Unlike humans who sweat to cool down, dogs rely on panting as their primary cooling mechanism.</p>
<p>Dr. Lisa Gunter, an expert in Animal Behavior &#038; Welfare and an assistant professor in the School of Animal Science at Virginia Tech, explains, “These physical limitations make breathing difficult, causing these breeds to be particularly susceptible to heatstroke more so than other dogs in surprisingly mild weather and humidity.” </p>
<figure id="attachment_430464" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430464" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-7.jpg" alt="Pets, especially brachycephalic breeds, are vulnerable to heatstroke due to their limited ability to cool down." width="1000" height="1333" class="size-full wp-image-430464" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-7.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-7-254x338.jpg 254w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-7-506x675.jpg 506w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-7-127x169.jpg 127w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-7-768x1024.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430464" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Pets, especially brachycephalic breeds, are vulnerable to heatstroke due to their limited ability to cool down.</figcaption></figure>
<p>When animals have trouble breathing, it can also reduce the oxygen in their blood. This puts extra strain on the hearts of brachycephalic dogs, making them more prone to heart issues, Gunter says.</p>
<p>“All dog owners, but especially those of brachycephalic dog breeds, have to be incredibly careful about the most quintessential dog-owner activity in extreme heat: dog walking,” she continues. </p>
<p>The type of sweltering heat that is more typical in the Southwest United States is spreading across North America as climate change worsens, which could turn deadly for these breeds.</p>
<p>“Owners of brachycephalic dogs need to be much more aware of the heat and its devastating effects than typical dog owners,” Gunter says. “Seemingly routine activities with brachycephalic dogs, like a mid-day potty break in the backyard or coming along on a car ride, can be deadly in extreme heat if they’re accidentally forgotten about or closed in a space without air conditioning for just a few minutes.”</p>
<p>A scientific study published in <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-020-66015-8" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Nature</a> further shows how Brachycephalic breeds like Bulldogs are at higher risk of heat-related illness due to their distinctive skull shape.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430466" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430466" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-5.jpg" alt="Increased heatwaves put pets at risk of overheating, leading to potentially life-threatening conditions." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-430466" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-5.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-5-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-5-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-5-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430466" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Increased heatwaves put pets at risk of overheating, leading to potentially life-threatening conditions.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>The Impact of Climate Change</h3>
<p>The warming climate poses a significant threat to these breeds. As temperatures rise, dogs are at risk of overheating and potentially collapsing or, in extreme cases, facing fatal heat-related illnesses. In the hottest year on record globally, the UK alone saw 395 dogs receive veterinary care for heat-related issues, with a 14% mortality rate, reports <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/photo-essays/2020-06-19/these-dog-breeds-are-the-most-vulnerable-to-heat-climate-change" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Bloomberg</a>.</p>
<h3>Ensuring Their Safety</h3>
<p>If you own or are considering adopting a brachycephalic breed, there are essential steps to protect them in the face of climate change:</p>
<h5>1. Be Mindful of the Heat</h5>
<p>Brachycephalic dogs should not be exposed to temperatures exceeding 91°F and 62% humidity, Dr. James A. Serpell, professor of Ethics &#038; Animal Welfare at the University of Pennsylvania School of Veterinary Medicine, told <a href="https://www.salon.com/2023/09/04/bulldogs-pugs-and-other-snout-less-dogs-will-suffer-as-climate-change-worsens-experts-warn/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Salon</a>. Pay attention to the weather and avoid outdoor activities during the hottest parts of the day.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430467" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430467" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-4.jpg" alt="Prolonged exposure to extreme heat can cause heat exhaustion and heatstroke in dogs and cats." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-430467" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-4.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-4-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-4-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-4-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430467" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Prolonged exposure to extreme heat can cause heat exhaustion and heatstroke in dogs and cats.</figcaption></figure>
<h5>2. Stay Hydrated</h5>
<p>Always carry water and a bowl for your dog, no matter how short the outing. Dehydration can exacerbate heat-related issues, reports the <a href="https://www.akc.org/expert-advice/health/keep-pug-frenchie-from-overheating/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">American Kennel Club</a>.</p>
<h5>3. Watch Their Weight</h5>
<p>Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial. Overweight brachycephalic dogs are more susceptible to heat-related problems, reports <a href="https://www.purinaproclub.com/resources/dog-articles/health/brachycephalic-research-shows-body-condition-is-key-to-thermoregulation" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Purina</a>.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430465" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430465" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-6.jpg" alt="Protecting pets from the impacts of climate change requires awareness, preparedness, and responsible ownership." width="1000" height="1777" class="size-full wp-image-430465" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-6.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-6-190x338.jpg 190w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-6-380x675.jpg 380w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-6-95x169.jpg 95w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-6-768x1365.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/pets-climate-change-6-864x1536.jpg 864w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430465" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Protecting pets from the impacts of climate change requires awareness, preparedness, and responsible ownership.</figcaption></figure>
<h5>4. Regular Exercise and Vet Check-ups</h5>
<p><a href="https://vcahospitals.com/know-your-pet/the-benefits-of-walking-your-dog" target="_blank" rel="noopener">VCA Hospitals</a> recommends engaging your dog in low-impact exercises and ensure regular veterinary check-ups to detect and address breathing problems. </p>
<h5>5. Adopt, Don&#8217;t Shop</h5>
<p>Consider <a href="https://www.animalhumanesociety.org/resource/brachy-whata-what-it-means-be-brachycephalic-dog" target="_blank" rel="noopener">adopting from rescues</a> if you&#8217;re thinking about bringing a brachycephalic breed into your family.</p>
<p>Climate change is a pressing concern, and our responsibility to safeguard the well-being of our four-legged companions is equally crucial. Let us unite to address the challenges of extreme heat and pollution forging a path toward a resilient and thriving society that prioritizes both human and pet health. Together, we can create a safer and more sustainable world for generations to come.</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Pepper was no ordinary dog. As a Chesapeake retriever, she was bred and trained by her owner, a seasoned sea-duck hunter, to fetch ducks from the calm waters off Toby’s Island near Port Medway, Nova Scotia. Pepper was on her second duck retrieval mission when tragedy struck.</p>
<p>Without warning, a large shark erupted from the depths, lifting Pepper into the air and pushing her beneath the water, <a href="https://ftw.usatoday.com/2023/10/hunter-shock-shark-attacks-dog-nova-scotia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">For The Win</a> reports. The dog&#8217;s owner could only watch helplessly.</p>
<p>“It happened so quickly and was so shocking that even though I was looking right at her when it happened, I cannot say for certain what type of shark it was,” the hunter said.</p>
<p>Pepper, miraculously resurfacing, struggled back to the boat, but her injuries were fatal, <a href="https://globalnews.ca/news/10036623/shark-attacks-dog-nova-scotia/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Global News</a> reports. </p>

<h3>Community Shock and Solidarity</h3>
<p>News of Pepper&#8217;s attack sent shockwaves through the community. Local residents took to social media to express their condolences and support for Pepper&#8217;s grieving owner. </p>
<p>While such occurrences are incredibly rare, they serve as a stark reminder of the untamed nature lurking just below the serene surface of our oceans. Tonya Wimmer, executive director of the Marine Animal Response Society, said the shark may have mistook Pepper for a seal.</p>
<p>“Assuming it was a white shark, it’s just doing what sharks do,” Wimmer told Global News.</p>

<h3>A Glimpse into Shark Behavior</h3>
<p>As local shark expert, Capt. Art Gaetan of Atlantic Shark Expeditions, told <a href="https://www.outdoorlife.com/survival/shark-attacks-hunting-dog/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Outdoor Life</a>, that based on Pepper&#8217;s owner&#8217;s estimation of its size, roughly 2.4 meters (just under 8 feet) long, the predator is believed to have been a juvenile Great White.</p>
<p>Sharks of this size typically inhabit these waters, and while Pepper didn&#8217;t become the shark&#8217;s meal, the bite proved to be fatal. </p>

<h3>Sharing a Cautionary Tale</h3>
<p>Pepper&#8217;s owner, who had been hunting sea ducks for years, had never heard of a dog being attacked by a shark during a duck retrieval mission. </p>
<p>“I was very close to shore, in about 20 feet of water, and my dog was only in the water for a matter of minutes,” he said. “To my knowledge, this is the first time a dog has been taken during a sea-ducking hunt, and it is certainly the first time I have witnessed the violence of such an attack so close to my boat.”</p>
<p>Marine life can be unpredictable and wild. While Pepper&#8217;s owner hopes that sharing his experience will lead to a better understanding of shark behavior and help others make safer choices in the ocean, the community, dog lovers, and ocean enthusiasts can only hope that such incidents remain exceedingly rare.</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Bret Michaels, the iconic frontman of the rock band Poison, recently made headlines by adopting a dog named after himself. This heartwarming story has garnered significant attention, and we&#8217;re here to shed light on this extraordinary act of compassion.</p>
<h3>A Heroic Canine Act</h3>
<p>The heartwarming tale began when a 6-year-old husky, fittingly named Bret Michaels, became an unexpected hero at the Nebraska Humane Society. The dog stepped up in a moment of crisis, offering a life-saving blood donation to a 4-week-old kitten suffering from flea anemia, <a href="https://people.com/bret-michaels-adopts-a-dog-named-named-after-him-exclusive-8364387" target="_blank" rel="noopener">PEOPLE</a> reports.</p>
<figure id="attachment_430439" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-430439" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bret-michaels-adopts-4.jpg" alt="Bret Michaels is a renowned American rock musician." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-430439" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bret-michaels-adopts-4.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bret-michaels-adopts-4-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bret-michaels-adopts-4-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/bret-michaels-adopts-4-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-430439" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Bret_Michaels_performs_in_Massapequa,_NY_in_2014.jpg" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Wikimedia Commons / Rjkowal</a><br />Bret Michaels is a renowned American rock musician.</figcaption></figure>
<p>The kitten was brought into the shelter as a stray, along with two other felines. However, one of the kittens exhibited concerning symptoms: it was &#8220;wobbly, weak, and lethargic,&#8221; indicating the presence of flea anemia, <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/entry/bret-michaels-adopts-dog-named-after-him_n_6534d63ae4b0689b3fbccbc5" target="_blank" rel="noopener">HuffPost</a> reports. Shelter staff recognized the urgency of the situation and decided to perform a blood transfusion, as waiting for blood type testing was not an option.</p>
<p>Uniquely, the shelter opted for xenotransfusion, a procedure in which dog blood is used to transfuse cats. This temporary measure, while unconventional, provided the kitten with a fighting chance. Fortunately, the initial transfusion began to show signs of improvement in the kitten&#8217;s condition.</p>
<p>“Cats, like humans, have blood types. Us humans are lucky to have a ‘universal donor’ type, O-, that can be safely transfused into a person with any other type,” wrote the Nebraska Humane Society, which noted that “canine blood can be transfused into felines” in an <a href="https://www.instagram.com/p/Cyg4oPZuDbe/?utm_source=ig_embed&#038;utm_campaign=loading" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Instagram</a> post.</p>

<h3>Bret Michaels, the Dog&#8217;s Hero</h3>
<p>This heartwarming story reached Michaels, the musician, who was deeply moved by the selfless act of the husky.</p>
<p>&#8220;I could not have been more touched and more honored to adopt Bret Michaels the husky, knowing that he gave blood to save the life of a kitten,&#8221; he told PEOPLE. &#8220;I could not work fast enough to adopt this husky and extend the awesome quality of little Bret Jr.&#8217;s life.&#8221;</p>
<p>Bret Michaels, the rockstar, immediately reached out to the Nebraska Humane Society, declaring his intention to adopt the heroic husky. His decision to welcome the dog into his family came at a time when he was still mourning the loss of his 14-year-old German shepherd, Phoenix, reports <a href="https://www.kare11.com/article/news/nation-world/bret-michaels-adopts-husky-named-bret-michaels-nebraska-humane-society/507-88e8800b-b156-41ae-a916-cf374ef8fb27" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Kare 11</a>.
