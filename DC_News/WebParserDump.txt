										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>The menace of &#8220;forever chemicals&#8221; is wreaking havok on our natural world.  While the impact of per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) on human health is well-documented, a <a href="https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0048969723045643" target="_blank" rel="noopener">recent study</a> has revealed the alarming toll these toxic compounds are taking on wildlife across the globe. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429367" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429367" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" fetchpriority="high" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-4.jpg" alt="PFAS, or per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, have profound effects on wildlife." width="1000" height="662" class="size-full wp-image-429367" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-4.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-4-511x338.jpg 511w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-4-255x169.jpg 255w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-4-768x508.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429367" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />PFAS, or per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, have profound effects on wildlife.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>PFAS: An Unseen Peril</h3>
<h5>What are PFAS?</h5>
<p><a href="https://www.niehs.nih.gov/health/topics/agents/pfc/index.cfm#:~:text=What%20Are%20PFAS%3F,ingredients%20in%20various%20everyday%20products." target="_blank" rel="noopener">PFAS</a>, or per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, are synthetic fluorinated chemicals that have been manufactured since the 1950s. Initially used for nonstick pans, their applications have grown exponentially, infiltrating our daily lives in food packaging, clothing, electronics, and more. These &#8220;forever chemicals&#8221; don&#8217;t break down easily, persisting in the environment for years and spreading far beyond their source.</p>
<h5>The Global Reach of PFAS</h5>
<p>A study published in <a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0048969723045643?via%3Dihub" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Science of The Total Environment</a> compiled existing data from scientific journals to create a global overview of PFAS exposure in wildlife. The results are chilling – PFAS contamination has been identified on every continent, with hotspots in the U.S., Europe, China, and Australia. These toxic compounds have infiltrated even the most remote corners of our planet, posing a dire threat to wildlife.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429366" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429366" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-5.jpg" alt="Studies have documented PFAS contamination in animals across the globe." width="1000" height="1500" class="size-full wp-image-429366" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-5.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-5-225x338.jpg 225w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-5-450x675.jpg 450w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-5-113x169.jpg 113w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-5-768x1152.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429366" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Studies have documented PFAS contamination in animals across the globe.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Wildlife on the Brink: The Alarming Impact of PFAS</h3>
<h5>Unseen Consequences</h5>
<p>While <a href="https://www.epa.gov/pfas/our-current-understanding-human-health-and-environmental-risks-pfas" target="_blank" rel="noopener">human health effects from PFAS</a> exposure are well-documented, the toll on wildlife remains underexplored. However, emerging research is raising red flags. PFAS have been found in the brains of both animals and humans, causing nervous system damage. Lab studies reveal impaired learning, memory issues, anxiety-like behavior, and diminished motor skills in animals exposed to PFAS, particularly affecting young animals&#8217; developing brains.</p>
<h5>Widespread Health Horrors</h5>
<p>The list of documented health impacts in wildlife is extensive and alarming, mirroring the effects observed in humans. As reported by the <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7906952/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">National Institutes of Health</a> PFAS exposure disrupts the immune system, interferes with reproduction and development, damages organs (including the liver), disrupts metabolism, induces tumors, and weakens immunity. In some cases, PFAS exposure has led to irreversible negative effects, including diminished health, reproduction, and survival.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429365" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429365" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-6.jpg" alt="PFAS exposure poses a severe risk to biodiversity." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-429365" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-6.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-6-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-6-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-6-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429365" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />PFAS exposure poses a severe risk to biodiversity.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>A Grave Concern for Endangered Species</h3>
<p>PFAS pose an existential threat to many endangered and vulnerable species. As the <a href="https://www.ewg.org/interactive-maps/pfas_in_wildlife/map/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Environmental Working Group</a> reports, these chemicals may not deliver instant death, but they erode health over time, increasing susceptibility to diseases and diminishing the chances of survival. The impacts of PFAS on rare and endangered species exacerbate the ongoing global biodiversity crisis, where approximately half of the world&#8217;s species are already under threat from various stressors.</p>
<h3>A Dark History, An Urgent Future</h3>
<h5>A History of Neglect</h5>
<p>Despite early awareness of PFAS&#8217;s toxicity, manufacturers kept this information from the public, reports the <a href="https://www.ucsf.edu/news/2023/05/425451/makers-pfas-forever-chemicals-covered-dangers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">University of California San Francisco</a>. The harmful effects on human health are now undeniable, but regulations to control PFAS remain woefully inadequate. It&#8217;s time to connect the dots between chemical regulation decisions and their devastating impact on global biodiversity.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429363" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429363" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-8.jpg" alt="Even remote regions, such as the Arctic, show PFAS contamination in wildlife." width="1000" height="1250" class="size-full wp-image-429363" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-8.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-8-270x338.jpg 270w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-8-540x675.jpg 540w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-8-135x169.jpg 135w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/PFAS-wildlife-8-768x960.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429363" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Even remote regions, such as the Arctic, show PFAS contamination in wildlife.</figcaption></figure>
<h5>A Global Response</h5>
<p>Around the world, PFAS regulations vary, with some countries taking decisive steps to ban certain compounds, <a href="https://int.anteagroup.com/news-and-media/blog/pfas-regulation-around-the-world" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Antea Group</a> reports. The study authors emphasize the urgent need for comprehensive protection of wildlife from chemical pollution as the global biodiversity crisis deepens.</p>
<p>The discovery of PFAS contamination in wildlife worldwide is a wake-up call. As we grapple with the ongoing challenges of climate change, habitat loss, and other environmental crises, the threat of &#8220;forever chemicals&#8221; adds yet another layer of complexity to our efforts to protect our planet. </p>
<p>Immediate action is essential to curb the spread of PFAS and mitigate their impact on both human and animal populations. We must heed this warning and join forces to safeguard the fragile balance of life on Earth. Click below and help us make a difference!</p>

<p>The post <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness/">7 Dog Breeds Prone To Deafness</a> appeared first on <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com">The Animal Rescue Site News</a>.</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Deaf Dog Awareness week is always the last full week of September. <a href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Deafness</a> in dogs is more common than you may realize, especially among certain breeds. </p>
<p>Dr. George M. Strain, former veterinarian and researcher at the School of Veterinary Medicine at Louisiana State University, <a href="https://www.lsu.edu/deafness/genetics.htm" rel="noopener" target="_blank">discovered</a>, &#8220;8% of all dogs in the US are bilaterally deaf and 22% are unilaterally deaf&#8221;.  </p>
<figure id="attachment_429371" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429371" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-1.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429371" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-1.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-1-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-1-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429371" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/dog-puppy-lapphund-love-cute-baby-2088424/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>Any dog can have congenital deafness (present at birth or shortly after) but breeds with white pigmentations are at a higher risk.</p>
<p>Multiple <a href="https://www.lsu.edu/deafness/incidenc.htm" rel="noopener" target="_blank">studies</a> confirm that dogs with merle and piebald coat pigmentations and blue eyes have a higher risk of deafness. Dogs with these coat colors often have white ears and lack pigment in the inner ear which causes hearing loss. </p>
<p>Learn more about the seven dog breeds prone to deafness below. </p>
<h3> Dalmatian</h3>
<figure id="attachment_429370" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429370" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-2.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429370" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-2.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-2-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-2-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429370" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/animals-dog-dalmatian-heterochromia-50712/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>30% of Dalmatians are deaf in either one or both ears. It is thought to be genetic and caused by the lack of pigmentation in their inner ear. DAS Dalmatians Sanctuary encourages people to learn more and <a href="https://www.dasdalmatians.co.uk/living-with-a-deaf-dalmatian" rel="noopener" target="_blank">said</a>, &#8220;Living with a deaf dog can be extremely rewarding and they are often quicker at picking things up than a hearing dog. Maybe because there are no noise distractions. They are very loving, responsive and manageable but you must always remember they can’t hear and you need to communicate exactly what you need with your actions and expressions&#8221;.</p>
<h3> Bull Terrier</h3>
<figure id="attachment_429357" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429357" style="width: 380px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-3-380x675.jpg" alt="" width="380" height="675" class="size-large wp-image-429357" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-3-380x675.jpg 380w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-3-190x338.jpg 190w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-3-95x169.jpg 95w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-3-768x1365.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-3-864x1536.jpg 864w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-3.jpg 1080w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429357" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/bull-terrier-dog-animal-white-112515/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>Deafness in both ears is seen in roughly 18% of white bull terriers. However, hearing loss can occur in any coat color. Boston Terrier World <a href="https://bullterrier.world/blogs/all/deafness-in-bull-terriers" rel="noopener" target="_blank">shared</a> on their website, &#8220;In Bull Terriers, the piebald gene is closely associated with deafness. Dogs with excessive white markings due to the piebald gene are more likely to have hearing impairments. This correlation is believed to be linked to the same genetic factors that control the development of the inner ear, as well as the distribution of pigmentation in the coat.&#8221;</p>
<h3> Australian Shepherd</h3>
<figure id="attachment_429356" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429356" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-4.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429356" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-4.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-4-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-4-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429356" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/nature-cute-animal-mammal-portrait-3210138/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>Double merle Australian shepherds are more likely to experience hearing loss and/or blindness than single merle dogs. Australian Shepherd Health &#038; Genetics Institute <a href="https://www.ashgi.org/home-page/genetics-info/color/deafness-white" rel="noopener" target="_blank">explains</a>, &#8220;The genes which determine white markings impact pigment cells wherever they occur in the body, not just in the coat. White on or near the ears is an indicator that pigment may be lacking in the inner ear as well. Pigment cells play an important part in the process of translating the mechanical vibration of a sound wave into the electrical impulse which travels via nerves to the brain. A tiny organ in the inner ear called a cochlea contains fluid and is lined with hair-like structures called cillia. When sound vibrates the air, those vibrations travel through the ear canal and vibrate the fluid which wiggles the cilia. The cilia connect with nerve endings through pigment cells. These cells translate the mechanical vibration of the cilia into electrical impulses that travel the nerves to the brain. If the pigment cells are absent, this translation can’t take place and the dog will be deaf in that ear.&#8221;  </p>
<h3>English Setter</h3>
<figure id="attachment_429355" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429355" style="width: 380px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-5-380x675.jpg" alt="" width="380" height="675" class="size-large wp-image-429355" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-5-380x675.jpg 380w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-5-190x338.jpg 190w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-5-95x169.jpg 95w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-5-768x1365.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-5-864x1536.jpg 864w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-5.jpg 1080w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429355" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/dog-english-setter-mammal-canine-6604583/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>The English Setter Association in the U.K. and America performed studies on the breed to try and determine what causes deafness. They both <a href="http://www.englishsetterassociation.co.uk/assets/files/deafness_survey.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">found</a> that puppies with a deaf parent had increased chances of being deaf. &#8220;The Americans also have data from 7 litters produced by Normal x Unilateral parents:</p>
<p>45 puppies tested 34 Normal hearing 75.60 %<br />
9 unilaterally deaf 20.00 %<br />
2 bilaterally deaf 4.40 %</p>
<p>24% of puppies were affected. 57% of litters had 1 or more affected puppy. It is clear from the American data that the incidence of both unilateral and bilateral deafness is much higher when one of the parents is unilaterally deaf, suggesting a genetic component to the condition.&#8221;</p>
<h3> Catahoula Leopard Dog</h3>
<figure id="attachment_429354" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429354" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-6.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429354" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-6.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-6-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-6-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429354" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/dog-pet-catahoula-leopard-dog-6695428/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>These dogs have stunning merle or brindle coats and striking blue eyes, which means they are more prone to deafness. They are otherwise very healthy and active dogs. PetMD <a href="https://www.petmd.com/dog/breeds/catahoula-leopard-dog" rel="noopener" target="_blank">states</a>, &#8220;The cause of this disorder is not completely understood, but there is a correlation between the dog’s coat color and deafness. Catahoula Leopard Dogs with mostly white fur may be more likely to inherit the disorder. The cause of this disorder is not completely understood, but there is a correlation between the dog’s coat color and deafness.&#8221;</p>
<h3> Australian Cattle Dog</h3>
<figure id="attachment_429353" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429353" style="width: 380px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-7-380x675.jpg" alt="" width="380" height="675" class="size-large wp-image-429353" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-7-380x675.jpg 380w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-7-190x338.jpg 190w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-7-95x169.jpg 95w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-7-768x1365.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-7-864x1536.jpg 864w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-7.jpg 1080w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429353" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://unsplash.com/photos/LxMb1ss0KaU" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Unsplash/ Bronty Woodard</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>A <a href="https://bmcvetres.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/1746-6148-8-202" rel="noopener" target="_blank">study</a> published by BMC Veterinary Research looked at how deafness impacts Australian Cattle Dogs. They found that 11% of the nearly 900 dogs tested were deaf in one or both ears. They concluded, &#8220;Australian Cattle Dogs suffer from CHSD (Congenital hereditary sensorineural deafness), and this disease is more common in dogs with mask-free faces, and in those without pigmented body patches.&#8221; They also discovered that it was more common in females. </p>
<h3>English Cocker Spaniel</h3>
<figure id="attachment_429351" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429351" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-8.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429351" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-8.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-8-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/dog-breeds-prone-to-deafness-8-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429351" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/cocker-spaniel-dog-lawn-canine-pet-7444312/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>Dr. Strain <a href="https://www.lsu.edu/deafness/incidenc.htm" rel="noopener" target="_blank">examined</a> solid and parti-colored English Cocker Spaniels to see how the pigmentation in the coat color played a role in deafness. Of the 1,510 parti-colored dogs examined, 84 or 5.6% were either deaf in one or both ears. The solid dogs had a lower occurrence of deafness with a total of 1.4%. </p>
<h3> Conclusion</h3>
<p>This list is not all inclusive. Congenital deafness has been found in over 106 breeds, <a href="https://www.lsu.edu/deafness/breeds.htm" rel="noopener" target="_blank">according</a> to Dr. Strain. </p>
<p>Don&#8217;t be afraid to adopt a deaf dog. Dogs cope extremely well with hearing loss and can do anything other dogs can do. As a bonus, they will remain calm during thunderstorms and fireworks. Please consider adopting a deaf dog or cat from your local shelter. </p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>The echoes of humanity&#8217;s nuclear legacy resonate far and wide, leaving an indelible mark on some of the most unsuspecting creatures across the globe. From the lush Pacific waters of Enewetak Atoll to the dense forests of Bavaria, the chilling consequences of nuclear testing and disasters are surfacing in the animal kingdom. </p>
<p>As <a href="https://www.nationalgeographic.com/animals/article/radioactive-animals-nuclear-testing-disasters " target="_blank" rel="noopener">National Geographic</a> reports, four animals in particular—Sea Turtles of Enewetak Atoll, Wild Boars of Bavaria, Reindeer of Norway, and Macaques of Japan—carry a radioactive legacy. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429341" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429341" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-10.jpg" alt="Chernobyl's fallout left a lasting radioactive legacy across Europe, impacting animals like reindeer in Norway." width="1000" height="1500" class="size-full wp-image-429341" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-10.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-10-225x338.jpg 225w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-10-450x675.jpg 450w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-10-113x169.jpg 113w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-10-768x1152.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429341" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Chernobyl&#8217;s fallout left a lasting radioactive legacy across Europe, impacting animals like reindeer in Norway.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Sea Turtles of Enewetak Atoll: Swimming through Radioactive Waters</h3>
<p><a href="https://www.bikiniatoll.info/history-of-enewetak/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Enewetak Atoll</a>, nestled between Australia and Hawaii, is a tropical paradise tainted by a tragic past. Following World War II, the United States conducted 43 nuclear weapons tests on this pristine atoll, subsequently sealing the radioactive waste in a concrete tomb. Recently, scientists have unveiled a disturbing revelation—the <a href="https://academic.oup.com/pnasnexus/article/2/8/pgad241/7244772" target="_blank" rel="noopener">radiation has found its way into the shells of sea turtles</a> inhabiting these waters.</p>
<p>The <a href="https://www.dtra.mil/Portals/61/Documents/NTPR/1982-DOE-EnewetakRadSpptProject-web-2.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">cleanup efforts initiated by the U.S.</a> in 1977 may have stirred up contaminated sediments within the atoll&#8217;s lagoon. These sediments, laden with radiation, were possibly ingested by the sea turtles or affected the algae and seaweed they feed on. </p>
<p>These turtles, found in various locations, bear witness to the far-reaching consequences of nuclear activity.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429340" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429340" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-11.jpg" alt="Radioactive elements can accumulate in plants, fungi, and lichens, which are then consumed by animals." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-429340" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-11.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-11-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-11-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-11-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429340" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Radioactive elements can accumulate in plants, fungi, and lichens, which are then consumed by animals.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Wild Boars of Bavaria, Germany: Unseen Fallout in the Forests</h3>
<p>In the idyllic forests of Bavaria, Germany, an unexpected menace lurks. Wild boars, a staple of European wildlife, sometimes carry <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2023/09/02/world/europe/germany-boars-radioactivity-study.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">astounding levels of radiation</a>. While scientists initially attributed this contamination to the Chernobyl disaster of 1986, recent research paints a different picture.</p>
<p>As the <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/climate-environment/2023/09/03/radioactive-wild-boar/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Washington Post</a> reports, Georg Steinhauser and his team discovered that up to 68 percent of radiation in Bavarian boars stems from global nuclear testing, spanning from Siberia to the Pacific. The contamination, marked by a unique nuclear signature, arises from truffle consumption, as these delicacies absorb radiation from nuclear fallout in the nearby ground, <a href="https://www.nationalgeographic.com/animals/article/radioactive-animals-nuclear-testing-disasters" target="_blank" rel="noopener">National Geographic</a> reports.</p>
<p>Steinhauser&#8217;s study unveiled shocking numbers, with wild boars harboring radiation levels that far exceeded safety limits. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429342" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429342" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-9.jpg" alt="Some animals, like sea turtles in Enewetak Atoll, accumulate radiation in their shells, reflecting the history of their environment." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-429342" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-9.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-9-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-9-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-9-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429342" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Some animals, like sea turtles in Enewetak Atoll, accumulate radiation in their shells, reflecting the history of their environment.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Reindeer of Norway: Chernobyl&#8217;s Lingering Legacy</h3>
<p>Chernobyl&#8217;s catastrophic fallout left an indelible mark on Europe, particularly Norway. The radioactive legacy of this disaster still lingers in patches across the country. Multiple <a href="https://www-pub.iaea.org/MTCD/Publications/PDF/Pub1239_web.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">studies show</a> radioactive raindrops deposited fallout, which was subsequently absorbed by mushrooms and lichens. These contaminated delicacies became fodder for herds of reindeer, setting off a cascade of radiation in the food chain.</p>
<p>While significant efforts have been made to mitigate the effects, some reindeer, particularly during years of abundant <a href="https://www.bfs.de/EN/topics/ion/environment/foodstuffs/mushrooms-game/mushrooms-game_node.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">wild mushroom growth</a>, can exhibit radiation levels that exceed safety standards. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429346" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429346" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-5.jpg" alt="Fukushima's nuclear accident in Japan resulted in elevated cesium levels in Japanese macaques." width="1000" height="782" class="size-full wp-image-429346" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-5.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-5-432x338.jpg 432w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-5-863x675.jpg 863w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-5-216x169.jpg 216w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-5-768x601.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429346" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Fukushima&#8217;s nuclear accident in Japan resulted in elevated cesium levels in Japanese macaques.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Macaques of Japan: Fukushima&#8217;s Impact on Primate Life</h3>
<p>In the wake of Japan&#8217;s <a href="https://world-nuclear.org/information-library/safety-and-security/safety-of-plants/fukushima-daiichi-accident.aspx" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant disaster</a> in 2011, Japanese macaques faced a disturbing reality. <a href="https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0068530" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Studies show</a> that cesium levels in these primates soared to alarming heights, peaking at 13,500 becquerels per kilogram. Research by suggests that the macaques absorbed this contamination through their diet, consuming buds, bark, mushrooms, and <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/223978119_Radionuclide_Analysis_on_Bamboos_following_the_Fukushima_Nuclear_Accident" target="_blank" rel="noopener">bamboo shoots</a>, all of which accumulate radioactive cesium from the soil.</p>
<p>The ramifications of such high cesium concentrations are profound. <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37180055/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Studies show</a> monkeys born after the accident may experience delayed growth and exhibit smaller heads, signifying the far-reaching impact of nuclear incidents on primate populations.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429345" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429345" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-6.jpg" alt="Radioactive contamination in wildlife can arise from nuclear testing, disasters, and fallout from various sources." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-429345" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-6.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-6-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-6-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/radioactive-animals-6-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429345" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Radioactive contamination in wildlife can arise from nuclear testing, disasters, and fallout from various sources.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Are These Animals Dangerous?</h3>
<p>While these stories are haunting, it&#8217;s crucial to note that the radiation these animals carry poses negligible threats to humans. Some, like the Fukushima macaques, are not consumed by humans, rendering them harmless. Others, such as sea turtles, bear such minuscule radiation levels that they are inconsequential. For animals like Bavarian boars and Norwegian reindeer, <a href="https://www.intechopen.com/chapters/50183" rel="noopener" target="_blank">stringent monitoring</a> ensures that contaminated meat never reaches consumers. </p>
<p>Nonetheless, these discoveries hold immense significance. They serve as potent reminders that nuclear fallout doesn&#8217;t merely vanish into the ether—it lingers in the ecosystems, intertwining the fates of unsuspecting creatures with humanity&#8217;s nuclear legacy.</p>
<p>As we marvel at the resilience of these animals, we must recognize the collective responsibility to mitigate the ongoing consequences of nuclear testing and disasters. Their tales serve as a poignant cautionary tale, pointing out our duty to ensure that the echoes of our nuclear past do not haunt the future of our planet. Click below to make a difference!</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Grizzly bears are fierce predators who rarely back down to another animal. However, there are instances when the bear&#8217;s survival instincts kick in and it realizes it is better to run than fight. </p>
<p>Bo Welden, a guide for <a href="https://www.jhecotouradventures.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Jackson Hole EcoTour Adventures</a>, witnessed a rare moment during one of his tours and captured it on video. He took to <a href="https://www.instagram.com/p/CnEzoWyLcMM/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Instagram</a> to share the amazing footage and asked, &#8220;What animal do grown grizzly bears run from?&#8221; </p>
<p>Some of his followers guessed another grizzly bear, pack of wolves, wolverine, moose, and even &#8220;Big Foot&#8221;, but they were all incorrect. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429318" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429318" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-2.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429318" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-2.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-2-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-2-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429318" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://www.nps.gov/media/photo/gallery-item.htm?pg=2566553&#038;id=621b262a-b983-4624-80b5-dd3a921931a5&#038;gid=F4D8E65C-1DD8-B71B-0B6D5153A5D3E5CB" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: NPS/Eric Johnston</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>The video opens with a shot of a grizzly bear who suddenly takes off running at full speed. The next animal you see is a charging mama bison who is chasing after the bear. </p>
<p>Welden explained what occurred in the video. He <a href="https://www.instagram.com/p/Cxb7zaKtHU_/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">wrote</a>, &#8220;Bear chased…..? A mother bison showed this grizzly bear that he is not the biggest baddest beast on the landscape. It was truly AMAZING to be able to witness this role reversal while on tour with @jacksonholeecotours with guests! We were stunned to see this moment in real time.&#8221;</p>
<p>There is nothing a mama bison wouldn&#8217;t do to protect her calf, including risking her own life to chase off a predator. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429319" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429319" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-3.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429319" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-3.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-3-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/grizzly-bear-chased-by-mama-bison-3-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429319" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: NPS/Jim Peaco</figcaption></figure>
<p>The video has gone viral, and people couldn&#8217;t resist commenting. That is so amazing! Their speed is always mind blowing.&#8221;</p>
<p>According to Yellowstone National Park&#8217;s website, <a href="https://www.nps.gov/yell/learn/nature/grizzlybear.htm" rel="noopener" target="_blank">grizzly bears</a> can reach speeds up to 40 mph and <a href="https://www.nps.gov/yell/learn/nature/bison.htm" rel="noopener" target="_blank">bison</a> fall just behind at 30 mph. </p>
<p>Another viewer <a href="https://www.instagram.com/p/Cxb7zaKtHU_/c/18010955200933304/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">made</a> a very wise observation, &#8220;If bears run from Bison you’d think the tourists at Yellowstone would stop trying to pet them <img src="https://s.w.org/images/core/emoji/14.0.0/72x72/1f926-1f3fc-200d-2640-fe0f.png" alt="🤦🏼‍♀️" class="wp-smiley" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;" />&#8221;. </p>

<p>The post <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs/">What Causes Hearing Loss in Dogs?</a> appeared first on <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com">The Animal Rescue Site News</a>.</p>
]]></description>
										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>The last full week of September is National Deaf Dog Awareness Week. Veterinarians shed light on the causes, challenges, benefits, and how to spot a dog experiencing hearing loss. </p>
<p>Deaf dogs make great pets who are trainable through visual cues and some even compete in dog sports. Read on to learn what causes hearing loss and signs your dog may be experiencing it. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429299" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429299" style="width: 675px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-3-675x675.jpg" alt="" width="675" height="675" class="size-large wp-image-429299" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-3-675x675.jpg 675w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-3-338x338.jpg 338w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-3-169x169.jpg 169w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-3-768x768.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-3.jpg 1000w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429299" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/dogs-poodle-white-boxer-pets-4429513/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<h3>What Causes Deafness in Dogs?</h3>
<p>American Kennel Club <a href="https://www.akc.org/expert-advice/health/what-you-should-know-about-canine-deafness/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">shares</a> that deafness in dogs is either inherited or acquired. &#8220;It is estimated that about 5 to 10 percent of dogs in the United States suffer from deafness, either in one ear (unilateral) or both ears (bilateral).&#8221;  There are many reasons why a dog may go deaf in one or both ears.</p>
<h4>Congenital Hearing Loss</h4>
<p> &#8211; Dogs with white, piebald and merle pattern coats have a higher chance of being born with congenital hearing loss. &#8220;The gene that causes Merle or Piebald coats affects eye color and skin pigment, usually presenting as mottled white patches of fur and blue eyes. Unpigmented skin in the inner ear can lead to nerve-ending atrophy and subsequent deafness in puppies during their early weeks of life,&#8221; <a href="https://longwoodvetcenter.com/your-deaf-dog-rocks/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">states</a> Longwood Veterinary Center. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429298" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429298" style="width: 675px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-2-1-675x675.jpg" alt="" width="675" height="675" class="size-large wp-image-429298" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-2-1-675x675.jpg 675w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-2-1-338x338.jpg 338w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-2-1-169x169.jpg 169w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-2-1-768x768.jpg 768w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-2-1.jpg 1000w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429298" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/puppy-young-dog-female-dog-3130116/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>It is inherited, irreversible, and spotted between 3-6 weeks of age. </p>
<p><a href="https://vcahospitals.com/know-your-pet/living-with-a-deaf-dog" rel="noopener" target="_blank">VCA Animal Hospitals</a> shared some dog breeds commonly affected by deafness from pigmentation: </p>
<ul>
<li>Australian Shepherds</li>
<li>Dalmatians</li>
<li>Great Danes</li>
<li>Welsh Corgis</li>
</ul>
<h4>Ear Wax Buildup</h4>
<p>A buildup of ear wax can cause temporary hearing loss. It can occur due to narrow ear canals found in dog breeds like Dalmatians and Boxers or dogs with hairy ears. </p>
<h4>Ear Infection</h4>
<p>Chronic ear infections can lead to temporary or permanent hearing loss in one or both ears. Some medicine used to treat infections can also damage the inner ear (rare) if there is a hole in the ear drum. It is called ototoxicity.  </p>
<figure id="attachment_429300" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429300" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-4.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429300" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-4.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-4-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-4-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429300" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/medicine-veterinary-equipment-5003631/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<h4> Trauma to Inner Ear</h4>
<p>Damage to the sensitive inner ear may occur from physical trauma. While not common, a blow to the head or ear can result in hearing loss in dogs. </p>
<h4>Loud Noises</h4>
<p>Prolonged exposure to loud noises over the dog&#8217;s life can also result in hearing loss known as presbycusis. Military, police, and hunting dogs are impacted the most. The loud noises damage the hair cells in the inner ear which can cause hearing loss. </p>
<h4> Obstruction</h4>
<p>Dogs manage to get into all sorts of things that they shouldn&#8217;t, so it should come as no surprise that foreign bodies in the ear canal can also cause temporary hearing loss. Once the object is removed, and if no damage has been done to the ear, the dog&#8217;s hearing will return to normal. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429310" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429310" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-5.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429310" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-5.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-5-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-5-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429310" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/dogs-chihuahua-pets-chihuahua-puppy-2693074/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<h4>Aging</h4>
<p>Hearing loss due to &#8220;old age&#8221; occurs slowly over time but is irreversible. But don&#8217;t worry, dogs adjust to the disability and are able to live normal lives. </p>
<h4>Cognitive disorder</h4>
<p>This is also referred to as &#8220;doggy Alzheimer&#8217;s&#8221;. This is also related to aging as senile dogs may show signs of hearing loss. Dr. Shawn Messonnier with Paws and Claws Animal Hospital <a href="https://pawsandclawsanimalhospital.com/5-causes-of-hearing-loss-in-dogs/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">states</a>, &#8220;I first &#8216;discovered&#8217; this condition, which is somewhat related to aging, almost 30 years ago when I first graduated from veterinary school. I noticed a number of aged dogs acting “senile”. Many of these senile dogs also had hearing loss and were hypothyroid.&#8221;</p>
<figure id="attachment_429311" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429311" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-6.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429311" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-6.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-6-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-6-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429311" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/old-man-pug-park-dog-character-2090377/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>There are several causes for hearing loss in dogs. The best way to care for your dog is to have them examined by a vet to discover the cause and treat accordingly. </p>
<h3>Signs Your Dog May Be Going Deaf</h3>
<p>Some dogs are born deaf, and it can be difficult to tell at first with rambunctious puppies. One way to test is with a squeaky toy or another high-pitched noise. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429314" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429314" style="width: 720px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-7.jpg" alt="" width="720" height="360" class="size-full wp-image-429314" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-7.jpg 720w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-7-600x300.jpg 600w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/what-causes-hearing-loss-in-dogs-7-300x150.jpg 300w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429314" class="wp-caption-text"><a href="https://pixabay.com/photos/corgi-dog-pet-pembroke-welsh-corgi-6744056/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Photo: Pixabay</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>Older dogs, just like people, may experience hearing loss. Dr. Kathryn Winger, assistant professor of veterinary neurology at the Michigan State University College of Veterinary Medicine, shared with <a href="https://www.petmd.com/dog/slideshows/6-causes-deafness-dogs" rel="noopener" target="_blank">PetMD</a> common signs dog experience when they are going through hearing loss. </p>
<ul>
<li>Lack of response to being called</li>
<li>No reaction to loud noises like the doorbell or other animals or people</li>
<li>Being easily startled or harder to arouse</li>
<li>Not interacting normally with littermates (for puppies)</li>
<li>Being extra rambunctious or vocal</li>
</ul>
<p>If your dog (no matter their age) does not respond to normal sounds, tilts or shakes its head frequently, or is displaying any of the above signs, make an appointment with your veterinarian. </p>
<h3> Deaf Dogs Up For Adoption</h3>
<p>While deaf dogs face unique challenges, they are highly adaptable and make amazing pets. Check out <a href="https://deafdogsrock.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Deaf Dogs Rock</a>. The inspiration behind the nonprofit was a deaf boxer named Nitro. This year alone, the nonprofit has helped save 145 deaf dogs from kill shelters. In addition to finding homes for deaf dogs through rescues across the U.S., Deaf Dogs Rock helps educate people on how to live with deaf dogs. Their mission is &#8220;to bring awareness to how much fun living with a deaf dog can be&#8221;. </p>

<p>While the salamander mussel may not capture headlines like other charismatic species, it plays a significant role in maintaining the health of our rivers and streams.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429291" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429291" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-5.jpg" alt="The salamander mussel is currently facing the risk of extinction." width="1000" height="663" class="size-full wp-image-429291" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-5.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-5-510x338.jpg 510w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-5-255x169.jpg 255w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-5-768x509.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429291" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:River_mussel_(35270993974).jpg" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Wikimedia Commons / Magnus Hagdorn</a><br />The salamander mussel is currently facing the risk of extinction.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>A Species in Peril</h3>
<p>The USFWS&#8217;s &#8220;<a href="https://www.fws.gov/node/5085286" target="_blank" rel="noopener">species status assessment</a>&#8221; indicates a grim reality. Over 80% of the 66 known populations of salamander mussels in the United States are at &#8220;high risk&#8221; due to various environmental threats. An additional 14% face moderate risks, including water contamination, changes in water flow, landscape alterations, invasive species, and risks to the mussel&#8217;s host species, the mudpuppy salamander.</p>
<h3>Unsung Ecosystem Engineers</h3>
<p>“Freshwater mussels are considered ‘silent sentinels’ of rivers and streams. Where mussels thrive, water quality tends to be good. Where mussels are declining, it’s an indication that rivers and streams they inhabit may be unhealthy,” the USFWS maintains. “In addition to being indicators of stream health, mussels can keep the water clean by filtering their food from the water, and with it, sediment and other pollutants.”</p>
<p>The USFWS has identified around 2,012 miles of rivers and streams as &#8220;critical habitat&#8221; for the salamander mussel. If these waterways receive the critical habitat designation, habitat restoration projects, such as stream bank stabilization and culvert replacements, may face added regulatory complexities. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429292" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429292" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-4.jpg" alt="The salamander mussel is a small freshwater bivalve." width="1000" height="750" class="size-full wp-image-429292" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-4.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-4-451x338.jpg 451w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-4-900x675.jpg 900w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-4-225x169.jpg 225w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-4-768x576.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429292" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Fresh_water_mussel_(4742432986).jpg" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Wikimedia Commons / U. S. Fish and Wildlife Service &#8211; Northeast Region</a><br />The salamander mussel is a small freshwater bivalve.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Conservation Efforts and Regulatory Considerations</h3>
<p>While the <a href="https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2023/08/22/2023-17668/endangered-and-threatened-wildlife-and-plants-endangered-species-status-for-salamander-mussel-and" target="_blank" rel="noopener">potential ESA designation</a> for the mussel&#8217;s habitat could introduce additional regulatory requirements, several conservation organizations are committed to collaborating with the USFWS to protect endangered species.</p>
<p>“In Virginia, the ESA listing for the Roanoke log perch helps shape the trout management protocols of the Virginia Department of Wildlife Resources,” Mark Taylor, Eastern Communications Director for Trout Unlimited (TU), told <a href="https://www.fieldandstream.com/conservation/feds-look-to-add-rare-salamander-mussel-to-endangered-species-list/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Field &#038; Stream</a>. “They’re not stocking predatory brown trout, for example, in watersheds that have those endangered fish.”</p>
<figure id="attachment_429290" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429290" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-6.jpg" alt="The United States Fish &#038; Wildlife Service (USFWS) has proposed federal protections for the salamander mussel." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-429290" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-6.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-6-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-6-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/salamander-mussel-6-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429290" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Healthy_Mussels_4511.jpg" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Wikimedia Commons / Chris Light</a><br />The United States Fish &#038; Wildlife Service (USFWS) has proposed federal protections for the salamander mussel.</figcaption></figure>

					<description><![CDATA[<p>Africa's white rhinos are making an astonishing comeback, defying extinction! </p>
<p>The post <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com/white-rhino-rebound/">The Remarkable Rebound of Africa&#8217;s White Rhino Population</a> appeared first on <a rel="nofollow" href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com">The Animal Rescue Site News</a>.</p>

										<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Africa&#8217;s iconic white rhino, facing the brink of extinction for years, is now staging a remarkable comeback. New data from the <a href="https://www.iucn.org/press-release/202309/african-rhino-numbers-are-increasing-despite-poaching" target="_blank" rel="noopener">International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN)</a> reveal that the continent&#8217;s white rhino population has rebounded for the first time in a decade, marking a significant milestone in the ongoing battle to protect these majestic creatures.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429307" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429307" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-4.jpg" alt="The white rhino is one of the world's largest land mammals." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-429307" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-4.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-4-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-4-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-4-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429307" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />The white rhino is one of the world&#8217;s largest land mammals.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>The Positive Numbers</h3>
<p>At the close of 2022, Africa was home to 23,290 rhinos in total, reflecting a notable 5.2% increase from the previous year, reports <a href="https://abcnews.go.com/International/africas-rhino-population-rebounds-1st-time-decade-new/story?id=103409810" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ABC News</a>. This encouraging trend is the result of dedicated conservation efforts across the continent, with both black and white rhino populations on the rise. According to <a href="https://www.wcpo.com/white-rhino-population-increases-for-first-time-in-over-a-decade" target="_blank" rel="noopener">WCPO</a>, black rhino populations surged by 4.2%, reaching a total of 6,487, while white rhino populations grew by an impressive 5.6%, totaling 16,803. This is particularly significant because it&#8217;s the first time since 2012 that the white rhino population has experienced an increase.</p>
<h3>A Near-Threatened Species</h3>
<p>The white rhino, classified as &#8216;near threatened&#8217; on the <a href="https://www.iucnredlist.org/species/4185/45813880" target="_blank" rel="noopener">IUCN&#8217;s Red List of threatened species</a>, has faced immense challenges over the years. These gentle giants, once numbering in the hundreds of thousands at the dawn of the 20th century, dwindled to a mere 23,290 by the end of 2022, according to <a href="https://www.iucn.org/press-release/202309/african-rhino-numbers-are-increasing-despite-poaching">IUCN data</a>. However, thanks to intensified protection and management initiatives, there is newfound hope for their survival.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429304" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429304" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-7.jpg" alt="Its name, "white rhino," is derived from the Afrikaans word "weit," meaning wide, describing its wide mouth." width="1000" height="667" class="size-full wp-image-429304" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-7.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-7-507x338.jpg 507w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-7-253x169.jpg 253w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-7-768x512.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429304" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Its name, &#8220;white rhino,&#8221; is derived from the Afrikaans word &#8220;weit,&#8221; meaning wide, describing its wide mouth.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>The Impact of Conservation</h3>
<p>Conservationists, wildlife organizations, and governments across Africa have worked tirelessly to protect these magnificent creatures.</p>
<p>&#8220;The rebounding rhino populations aren&#8217;t just a win for rhinos, but for the many wildlife species that share their ecosystems and the people who steward these lands,&#8221; Nina Fascione, Executive Director at the International Rhino Foundation, told ABC News.<br />
&#8220;The growing rhino populations are a testament to the effectiveness of collaborative conservation efforts throughout Africa and the resilience of these species.&#8221;</p>
<h3>The Ongoing Threat of Poaching</h3>
<p>Despite these positive gains, poaching remains a critical threat to rhinos. The <a href="https://rhinos.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/State-of-the-Rhino-2023_Final_Updated-9212023-.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">2023 State of the Rhino</a> report highlights the changing tactics of poachers, who have shifted their focus from the largest rhino populations to more vulnerable ones. </p>
<p>As <a href="https://www.nationalgeographic.com/animals/article/rhino-numbers-drop-kruger-national-park" target="_blank" rel="noopener">National Geographic</a> reports, South Africa, home to the continent&#8217;s largest rhino population, has suffered significant losses due to poaching, forcing large protected areas like Kruger National Park to enhance security measures. In response, poachers have turned their attention to smaller areas like the Hluhluwe Imfolozi Game Reserve, <a href="https://news.mongabay.com/2022/10/poaching-surges-in-the-birthplace-of-white-rhino-conservation/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Mongabay</a> reports.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429302" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429302" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-9.jpg" alt="Despite rebounding populations, poaching for rhino horns remains a major threat." width="1000" height="1500" class="size-full wp-image-429302" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-9.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-9-225x338.jpg 225w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-9-450x675.jpg 450w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-9-113x169.jpg 113w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-9-768x1152.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429302" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Despite rebounding populations, poaching for rhino horns remains a major threat.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Namibia, home to the world&#8217;s largest black rhino population, experienced a staggering 93% increase in rhino poaching from 2021 to 2022, reports the <a href="https://www.savetherhino.org/rhino-species/black-rhino/poaching-in-namibia-almost-doubles-in-2022/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Save the Rhino</a>. In response, the U.S. and South Africa launched a joint anti-poaching taskforce, aiming to disrupt illicit trade and support law enforcement efforts.</p>
<h3>The Role of Climate Change</h3>
<p><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6334905/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Studies show</a> that climate change poses an additional threat to Africa&#8217;s rhino population. Its impacts on human communities can lead to <a href="https://www.worldwildlife.org/stories/what-is-human-wildlife-conflict-and-why-is-it-more-than-just-a-conservation-concern" target="_blank" rel="noopener">human-wildlife conflict</a>, increasing the risk of encounters between rhinos and humans. Competition over dwindling water resources may also escalate conflicts between communities and wildlife. As communities grapple with <a href="https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg2/chapter/chapter-8/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">poverty resulting from crop and livestock losses due to climate change</a>, poaching can become an alternative means of income.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429305" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429305" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-6.jpg" alt="Among all rhino species, southern white rhinos are the only ones not classified as endangered." width="1000" height="613" class="size-full wp-image-429305" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-6.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-6-551x338.jpg 551w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-6-276x169.jpg 276w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/white-rhino-rebound-6-768x471.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429305" class="wp-caption-text">Photo: Pexels<br />Among all rhino species, southern white rhinos are the only ones not classified as endangered.</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Protecting a Precious Legacy</h3>
<p>The story of Africa&#8217;s white rhinos serves as a beacon of hope amid the challenges of conservation.</p>
<p>Rhino populations have persevered for millions of years, and it is our collective responsibility to ensure they do not face extinction on our watch.</p>
<p>As we celebrate the success story of Africa&#8217;s white rhinos, let us also renew our commitment to protect these incredible creatures, ensuring that they continue to roam the African landscape for generations to come. </p>
<p>By standing together, we can make a difference and help preserve the legacy of these ancient and majestic creatures, ensuring they thrive in the wild once more. The rebound of Africa&#8217;s white rhino population is a victory for conservation and a testament to the resilience of these magnificent beings.</p>

<p>Maya&#8217;s favorite part of summer was the sunshine and sunbathing.</p>

<p>Living in the northeast, we have only a few months of the warm weather, and Maya would take full advantage of every warm minute.</p>
<hr/>
<p><a href="https://blog.theanimalrescuesite.greatergood.com/share-your-rescue-story/?gg_medium=content&#038;gg_source=ars&#038;gg_campaign=rescue-story&#038;gg_content=local" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><center>SHARE YOUR OWN RESCUE STORY</center></strong></a></p>
<hr/>
<p>She&#8217;d lay in the sun at every opportunity. When outside, she&#8217;d usually lay in the dirt, because the dirt was cool, sun was hot &#8211; our girl was smart. Inside, her favorite sunny spot was the sliding door in the back of the house, with the curtains covering her.</p>
<figure id="attachment_429282" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429282" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails1.jpg" alt="" width="1000" height="1333" class="size-full wp-image-429282" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails1.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails1-254x338.jpg 254w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails1-506x675.jpg 506w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails1-127x169.jpg 127w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails1-768x1024.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429282" class="wp-caption-text">PHOTO: JOANN WILLIAMS</figcaption></figure>
<p>During cooler temps, she would move around the house finding the sunbeam to lay in. Summertime also meant that her fur friends were outside more, which meant daily doggy play dates &#8211; which included lots of rounds of zoomies through the yard. </p>
<p>She was the social butterfly of the neighborhood, greeting humans and dogs on every walk. We would say she was the Norm from &#8220;Cheers&#8221; of the neighborhood &#8211; everyone knew her and loved her. </p>
<figure id="attachment_429283" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-429283" style="width: 1000px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails2.jpg" alt="" width="1000" height="750" class="size-full wp-image-429283" data-srcset="https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails2.jpg 1000w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails2-451x338.jpg 451w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails2-900x675.jpg 900w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails2-225x169.jpg 225w, https://d1dd4ethwnlwo2.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mayatalesofsummertails2-768x576.jpg 768w" data-sizes="(min-width:1200px) 1200px, (min-width:992px) 992px, (min-width:768px) 768px, (min-width:576px) 480px, 300px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-429283" class="wp-caption-text">PHOTO: JOANN WILLIAMS</figcaption></figure>
<p>Maya&#8217;s life was filled with many ailments, from neck and leg issues to respiratory issues and cancer. It would seem after one issue would resolve, she&#8217;d have a few months reprieve, then another issue would present. Every one of them, she (and we) faced head on, and she kept being her happy, lovable self. Maya the warrior. </p>
